: Roger Moffat: Well, about ten years ago | had a heart by-pass operation and, about the same

Interviewer: And what kind of success did you have in the early days?

Roger Moffat: You could say it was a bit like taking a roller coaster ride and wondering when you
were going to come flying off at break-neck speed! Everything was a challenge:
finance, production, marketing.

Interviewer: But that’s all in the past, you're ... you’re apparently much sought after now. I hear
forthcoming film productions are queuing up for your services.

Roger Moffat: Some — yes. There’s no doubt that we're certainly growing rapidly but we’re still
small, and | think it’s probably important to remain that way. I’ve seen too many
organisations just grow and grow and in the end they finish up over-reaching
themselves — stretching themselves to the limit.

Interviewer: Do you have any regrets about the way things have gone? — About the way your
life has taken a different turn?

Roger Moffat: To be honest, none at all. | feel that I've escaped being a slave to a regular
income, from commuting, from having to justify my actions to everyone, from
having to attend the office party, from having to book my holidays in advance —
actually, | don’t have any holidays at all at the moment, come to think of it. I’m too
busy! But best of all, I’ve nothing to do with office politics!

Interviewer: Probably the biggest advantage of all! So, what's the secret of your meteoric rise?

Roger Moffat: Oh, | couldn’t have done anything without the support of my wife, Lili, who’s also
my business partner, and there’s our two daughters, of course, Natasha and
Katia. They’ve all been wonderful.

Interviewer: So what kind of job did you start out doing?

Roger Moffat: | graduated in mechanical engineering and then spent about 20 years in industry.
Then my job — | was the chief engineer in an air-conditioning firm — just
disappeared overnight. Anyway, after that, | set up my own computer-aided
system that makes really intricate architectural models.

Interviewer: And you also supply components for the aerospace industry, don’t you?

Roger Moffat: We do, but | have to admit that it’s the film work that really interests me most.

Interviewer: Do you worry about the future?

Roger Moffat: No more than anyone else. | mean, there’s no job security anywhere these days,
is there? Of course, it’s a risk running your own company, but then you’re equally
as vulnerable staying employed. | decided it was safer to be in charge of my own
show than to be a part of someone else’s. Naturally, I've had problems. We had to
sell the family house, the one | built myself. But, looking back, it all seems worth it.
| was always infuriated by having to justify myself to people whom | didn’t consider
to be my intellectual superiors!

Interviewer: How would you describe yourself? What are your strengths, weaknesses?

Roger Moffat: | think ’'m a bit of an oddball character really. | suppose you might say that | was a
hard-headed romantic. | believe that an engineer has to invent ideas. You need to
be very talented. You need to have a feeling for balance and form. You also need
to feel you have status and that people value what you're doing.
